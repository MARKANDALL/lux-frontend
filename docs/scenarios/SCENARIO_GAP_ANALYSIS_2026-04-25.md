### Difficulty gaps — what's NOT covered

1. **Accent comprehension** — understanding different English accents (British, Australian, Indian, Southern US, etc.). This is one of the biggest real-world challenges for learners. Zero coverage.
2. **Speed of speech** — the other person talks too fast. Very common difficulty. Zero coverage.
3. **Cultural differences in communication style** — directness vs indirectness, personal space, eye contact norms. Zero coverage.
4. **Numbers, dates, and measurements** — giving and receiving numerical information (addresses, prices, times, quantities). Partially covered in calling (keeping track) but not as a standalone difficulty.
5. **Reading and responding to written information in real time** — menus, signs, forms, screens. Partially touched (banking paperwork, shopping sizes) but not as a communication difficulty.
6. **Interrupting or being interrupted** — knowing how and when to break in. Only covered in videocall. Not covered in face-to-face settings.
7. **Saying no / declining** — covered in shopping (offers) but not in social, professional, or authority contexts.
8. **Asking for more time to think** — stalling phrases, buying time. Zero coverage.
9. **Code-switching between formal and informal** — mid-conversation register shifts. Zero coverage as a difficulty (covered as objective in student/mail, but not as something that's hard).
10. **Mishearing / acoustic confusion** — homophones, similar-sounding words, mumbling. Only "road noise" and "noisy environment" touch this.
11. **Giving bad news or difficult information** — calling covers relaying medical info, but not the broader skill of delivering unwelcome news.
12. **Receiving criticism without becoming defensive** — school partially covers "receiving mixed feedback" but not the broader pattern.
13. **Phone-specific difficulties** — only 1 phone scenario. Missing: poor connection, being put on hold, automated menus, leaving voicemail, conference calls.
14. **Digital communication literacy** — texting conventions, email tone, chat etiquette. Zero coverage beyond videocall.
15. **Group conversations (3+ people)** — every scenario is 1-on-1 (or 1-on-couple). No group dynamics.

## 10. How to Use This Document

When adding new scenarios:
1. Check this document's gap lists first
2. Prioritize gaps from the Top 10
3. Ensure the new scenario adds at least one difficulty and one objective not already well-covered
4. Check demographic balance (age, gender, and when portrait tracking exists, ethnicity and wealth)
5. Consider communication channel — strongly favor non-face-to-face channels until balanced
6. Update this document after additions

When editing existing scenarios:
1. Check if the edit introduces a difficulty or objective that fills a gap — bonus
2. Check if the edit removes coverage of something unique — flag it
3. Maintain desc vs more distinctness (no redundancy)
